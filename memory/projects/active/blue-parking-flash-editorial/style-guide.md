# blue-parking-flash-editorial style guide

> Project-level style rules. If these conflict with general `real-human` preferences, this project guide wins.

## Must Keep

- Adult female model, clearly 22+; fashion editorial, not teen-coded.
- Bright outdoor retail parking lot or loading-bay environment.
- Blue painted floor, grey concrete, black-yellow striped safety poles, generic blue/yellow retail signage with no readable brand.
- Direct flash on the subject plus hard midday sun, with crisp shadow geometry.
- Long peach-blonde or rose-gold wavy hair, glossy makeup, soft doll-like styling without copying a specific face.
- Dusty-blue cropped tube top, beige utility shorts, pale blue belt, chunky white sneakers, slouchy socks or leg warmers.
- Silver/black bracelets, rings, small necklace, optional hair clip.
- Poses should feel like a continuous fashion-photo set: pole lean, salute, curb sit, cart recline, selfie wink, kneel.

## Composition Logic

- Use foreground scale: shoes, phone, shopping cart rail, or hand can sit close to the lens.
- Use location lines: parking stripes, curb seams, cart rails, canopy beams, and lane arrows should lead back to the face.
- Keep the face readable and near the upper third or central vertical axis.
- Let the body create S-curves and diagonals rather than flat standing poses.
- Anchor each frame to one prop so it does not become generic street photography.
- Crop like a phone image: vertical frame, slight high-angle snapshot, occasional tilted horizon.

## Background Rules

- Background should feel like a real service area: parked cars, planters, cart bay, delivery truck, low retail facade.
- Blue/yellow color blocks can echo the reference, but never render readable real store names.
- Include weathered textures: chipped paint, rust on beams, scuffed poles, cracked blue ground.
- Avoid overly clean studio backgrounds.

## Interface Layer

- Default: no UI, no search overlay, no hashtags, no watermark.
- Optional screenshot mode: top-left translucent search capsule and small bottom-left yellow tag text can be added only when explicitly requested.

## Fail Conditions

- Looks underage or teen-coded.
- Real brand text, store logo, watermark, screenshot UI, or random readable text appears.
- Hands, bracelets, rings, cart wires, or shoe geometry are deformed.
- Lighting becomes soft studio glamour instead of hard outdoor sun plus flash.
- Outfit drifts away from the blue crop top / beige shorts / white chunky sneaker system.
- Background loses the parking-lot identity.
